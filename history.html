History of Antipolo

1. Location: Antipolo is a city in the province of Rizal on the island of Luzon, Philippines.

2. Geography: It is about 6 meters above sea level, bordered by San Mateo and La Paz (north), Calumpit (west), Quezon City and Pasig (south), and Manila Bay (east).

3. Origin of Name: The city’s name comes from the Tipolo (breadfruit) tree, which was once abundant in the area.

4. Founding: Antipolo was established in 1570 by Miguel de Legazpi, the first governor-general of the Philippines, as a religious retreat and exploration base.

5. Spanish Fortress: The Spanish built a military fortress in Antipolo to protect against Moro attacks.

6. Christian Mission (1571): Fray Andres de Urdaneta arrived to spread Christianity, starting on November 6 and finishing on December 20, 1571, baptizing 221 natives and establishing six missions.

7. Second Mission (1572): Fray Juan Crespi joined Urdaneta to establish another mission named San Pablo de Tiaong on June 5, 1572.

8. Religious Influence: The Franciscan missionaries first built a church in 1578, later replaced by Jesuits such as Fr. Chirino and Fr. Almarique.

9. Christianization: By 1601, Antipolo’s Christian population grew to 3,000, including Dumagats, Tagals, Indians, and Aetas.

10. Province of Rizal (1901): On June 11, 1901, Antipolo joined the newly created Province of Rizal.

11. Municipal Changes (1903–1919): 1903: Antipolo merged with Boso-Boso and Teresa. 1913: The sitios of Mayamot and Bulao were added. 1919: Teresa separated to become its own municipality.

12. Railway (1908): The Manila Railroad Company opened a train line to Antipolo on December 24, 1908.

13. Capital of Rizal: Antipolo became the capital of Rizal Province in 1901.

14. Cityhood (1998): Through Republic Act No. 8508, signed by President Fidel V. Ramos on April 4, 1998, Antipolo became a component city after a plebiscite.

15. First City Mayor: Angelito Gatlabayan became Antipolo’s first city mayor after cityhood.

16. Religious Significance: Known as the “Pilgrimage City”, it is famous for the Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage icon.
